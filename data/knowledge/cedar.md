# Cedar Shake & Shingle Roof System

## Overview
Natural cedar roofing — either cedar shakes (thicker, hand-split or machine-split) or cedar shingles (thinner, machine-sawn). Common in the Roaring Fork Valley for mountain aesthetic. Cedar requires proper ventilation and underlayment for longevity at altitude.

## Underlayment
- **OC Titanium PSU 30** — ALWAYS (per universal rules)
- **SolarHide Radiant Barrier** ($340/roll) — ALWAYS for cedar (non-metal), IN ADDITION to PSU 30
- Cedar may also require breather underlayment or spaced sheathing for proper ventilation — note to user if relevant
- Do NOT select GAF VersaShield (metal only)

## Fasteners

- **1.25" Plasticap Pail** ($40/pail) — For underlayment

  - Same calculation: eave_length × rows_by_pitch
  - Rows: 1-4/12 = 1, 5-7/12 = 2, 8-10/12 = 3, 11-12/12 = 4
- Labor: **Snowguard Install** ($5.00/ea)

## Special Considerations for Aspen/High Altitude
- Cedar at 8,000'+ elevation needs excellent ventilation to prevent moisture damage
- Stainless steel fasteners are strongly recommended (galvanized can stain cedar)
- Fire treatment may be required by local code — check Pitkin County requirements
- Cedar has a shorter lifespan at altitude than in coastal/lowland areas — worth mentioning to clients

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Do NOT select GAF VersaShield (metal only)
- Do NOT use galvanized nails if stainless is available (staining risk)
- Do NOT select snow fence (that's for metal roofs only)
